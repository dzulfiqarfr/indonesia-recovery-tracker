Economic growth

Indonesia’s economy plunged into a recession in the third quarter of 2020, the country’s first recession since the 1998 Asian Financial Crisis. The economy shrank as household consumption and investment contracted due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Most sectors showed declines, with a few exceptions such as the information and communication sector. But there are signs of recovery.

Overall

Gross domestic product (GDP) (percent change from a year earlier)

The country’s GDP contracted by -0.74 percent year-on-year (yoy) in the January-March period, less severe than the previous quarter.

See also community mobility

The pandemic has led the GDP to contract by 5.32 percent yoy in the second quarter of 2020 and pushed it to the first recession in two decades as it contracted by 3.49 percent yoy in the third quarter of last year.

Contribution to growth

By expenditure (percentage points)

Tips: click the legend item to select or deselect a trace.

The private consumption contributed -1.28 percentage points and the investment 0.33 percentage points to the annual GDP growth in the January-March period.

The net exports and government consumption offset the contractions in other expenditure components by contributing 0.44 and 0.17 percentage points, respectively.

Documentation Description: the contribution to growth is a component’s growth weighted by its share in the GDP at constant 2010 prices in the previous quarter.
