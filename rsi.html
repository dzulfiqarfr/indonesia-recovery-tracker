Retail sales

Retail sales have been declining and unusually low during Ramadan, Idul Fitri, Christmas and year end due to the COVID-19 pandemic. While retail sales across categories remain below the usual level before the pandemic, some categories have shown signs of improvements.

Note: April 2021 figure is an estimate.

The Retail Sales Index was estimated to rise by 9.79 percent year-on-year (yoy) to 209.33 in April, less severe than the previous month.

See also community mobility

The pandemic has led the index to fall by its steepest decline at 20.61 percent yoy to 198.3 in May 2020, when Ramadan and Idul Fitri took place.

Note

The Retail Sales Index is based on a monthly survey from Bank Indonesia. The central bank has conducted the survey since September 1999.

Since January 2015, the survey covers around 700 retailers in 10 cities, comprising Jakarta, Semarang, Bandung, Surabaya, Medan, Purwokerto, Makassar, Manado, Banjarmasin and Denpasar.
