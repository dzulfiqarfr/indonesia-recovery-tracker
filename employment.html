Employment

The COVID-19 pandemic has forced millions of workers out of their job, sending the unemployment rate to a level the country last seen in 2011. The formal sector took a harder hit than the informal sector.

Unemployment

Rate

(percent)

Workers

(millions)

The number of unemployed workers rose by 2.66 million to 9.77 million in August 2020 from a year earlier. As a result, the unemployment rate was up by 1.84 percentage points to 7.07 percent over the same period. The unemployment rate in August 2020 was the highest in a decade.

See also inflation

Unemployment rate by province

(percent)

The highest increase in the unemployment rate was seen in Jakarta at 4.41 percentage points, which brought the share of unemployed workers in the province to 10.95 percent in August 2020.

See also poverty by province

North Maluku recorded the smallest increase in the unemployment rate at 0.34 percentage points yoy to 5.15 percent in August 2020.

Employment by status

The share of employees in the labor force showed the steepest annual decline at -4.28 percentage points to 36.37 percent in August 2020. The number of employees was down by -10.74 percent yoy to 46.72 million.

Employment by sector

The steepest annual decline in the share of workers was recorded at -1.3 percentage points in the manufacturing sector, which brought the share to 13.61 percent in August 2020. The number of workers in the sector was down by -8.93 percent yoy to 17.48 million over the period.

See also economic growth by sector

Meanwhile, the agriculture, forestry and fishery sector recorded a 7.83 percent yoy increase in the number of workers to 38.22 million, leading to an increase of 2.22 percentage points yoy in the share of workers to 29.76 percent.

Note

The data is based on the National Labor Force Survey (Sakernas) from BPS, which takes a sample of 75,000 households in the February round and 300,000 households in the August round. Read more about the data here.

The International Labor Organization (ILO) has replaced unpaid family workers with contributing family workers, namely those workers who hold self-employment jobs as own-account workers [] in a market-oriented establishment operated by a related person living in the same household.
