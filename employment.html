Employment

The COVID-19 pandemic has forced millions of workers out of their job, sending the unemployment rate to a level the country last seen in 2011. The formal sector took a harder hit than the informal sector.

Unemployment

Rate

The number of unemployed workers rose by 1.82 million to 8.75 million in February 2021 from a year earlier. As a result, the unemployment rate was up by 1.32 percentage points to 6.26 percent over the same period. The unemployment rate in August 2020 was the highest in a decade.

Employment by status

The share of employees in the labor force showed the steepest annual decline at -2.66 percentage points to 37.02 percent in February 2021. The number of employees was down by -8.25 percent yoy to 48.52 million.

Employment by sector

The steepest annual decline in the share of workers was recorded at -0.52 percentage points in the government, defense and social security administration sector, which brought the share to 3.55 percent in February 2021. The number of workers in the sector was down by -14.31 percent yoy to 4.66 million over the period.

See also economic growth by sector

Meanwhile, the wholesale and retail trade; car and motorcycle repair and maintenance sector recorded a 1.57 percent yoy increase in the number of workers to 25.16 million, leading to an increase of 0.61 percentage points yoy in the share of workers to 19.2 percent.

Note

The data is based on the National Labor Force Survey (Sakernas) from BPS, which takes a sample of 75,000 households in the February round and 300,000 households in the August round. Read more about the data here.

The International Labor Organization (ILO) has replaced unpaid family workers with contributing family workers, namely those workers who hold self-employment jobs as own-account workers [] in a market-oriented establishment operated by a related person living in the same household.

BPS started in August 2020 adding the data on workers affected by COVID-19, namely those who have been laid off or furloughed in the period starting from February 2020. Aside from workers getting unemployed and facing reduced working hours, the agency also recorded other pandemic impacts on the labor force:

  • Not in the workforce: people aged 15 or above who still go to school, take care of their household or have other non-personal activities. Working age population who have stopped working and are out of the workforce in the period starting from February 2020 belong to this group.

  • Furloughed: workers who have not been working in the past week for various reasons. People who have jobs but have not been working temporarily in the period starting from February 2020 belong to this group.
