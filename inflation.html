Inflation

The inflation rate has remained low and below Bank Indonesia’s target since the middle of 2020, around two months after the COVID-19 pandemic started battering the country.

The monthly inflation rate was recorded at 0.08 percent in March, lower than the rate in the same month in the previous year.

On an annual basis, the inflation rate stood at 1.38 percent in February, lower than the rate in the same month a year earlier.

The pandemic has led to a record-low full-year annual inflation rate at 1.68 percent in 2020.

By component

Monthly inflation (percent)

The monthly core inflation rate was recorded at 0.11 percent in February, lower than the level in the same month in the previous year.

For administered prices, the monthly inflation rate stood at 0.21 percent, higher than a year earlier. For volatile prices, the monthly deflation stood at -0.01 percent, lower than a year earlier.

By expenditure group

Monthly inflation (percent)

The highest monthly inflation rate was seen in the household equipment, tools and routine maintenance at 0.36 percent in February. It was followed by transportation and food and beverage services or restaurants expenditure groups.

Note

BPS builds the Consumer Price Index based on the 2018 Cost of Living Survey, which covered 90 cities and 853 commodities. Of the commodities, 711 were core components, 101 were volatile prices and the rest were government-administered prices.

Bank Indonesia has set a target of maintaining the annual inflation rate between 2 and 4 percent.
