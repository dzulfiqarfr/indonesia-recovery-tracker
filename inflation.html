Inflation

Consumer price inflation has stay muted and below Bank Indonesia’s target since the middle of 2020, around two months into the COVID-19 pandemic.

Overall

Monthly

Annual

The Consumer Price Index (CPI) posted a monthly deflation of -0.16 percent in June. On an annual basis, the inflation rate stood at 1.33 percent.

See also unemployment

In 2020, the pandemic led to a record-low full-year annual inflation rate at 1.68 percent.

By component

CPI (percent change from a month earlier)

The monthly core inflation rate was recorded at 0.14 percent in June.

For administered prices, the monthly deflation rate stood at -0.21 percent. For volatile prices, the monthly deflation rate stood at -1.23 percent.

By expenditure group

CPI (percent change from a month earlier)

The highest monthly inflation rate was seen in the personal care and other services at 0.35 percent in June. It was followed by food and beverage services or restaurants and recreation, sports and culture expenditure groups.

Note

BPS builds the CPI based on the 2018 Cost of Living Survey, which covered 90 cities and 853 commodities. Of the commodities, 711 were core components, 101 were volatile prices and the rest were government-administered prices.

Bank Indonesia has set a target of maintaining the annual inflation rate between 2 and 4 percent.
